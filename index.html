Project overview

About PowerCast

PowerCast is an AI-powered electricity load forecasting system built to tackle Pakistan's chronic load-shedding crisis using real historical demand data, statistical modeling, and time-series machine learning.

Time Series ARIMA Pakistan Grid NEPRA Data Load Forecasting Policy Research

The problem

Pakistan experiences some of the world's most severe load-shedding — sometimes 10–12 hours daily in rural areas. The root cause is not just a supply deficit, but poor demand forecasting. Without knowing when and where electricity is needed most, generation scheduling is reactive, wasteful, and unreliable.

⚡

10–12 hrs daily outages

Rural Pakistan faces severe load-shedding, disrupting agriculture, education, and small businesses.

📉

Billions lost annually

Pakistan's economy loses an estimated $4–6 billion per year due to unplanned power outages.

🔁

Reactive scheduling

Grid operators currently respond to outages after they occur — not before. AI forecasting flips this.

🌱

Fossil fuel dependence

Over 70% of Pakistan's electricity comes from fossil fuels — inefficient and expensive to scale rapidly.

The PowerCast solution

PowerCast trains two ML models on 30+ years of Pakistan electricity data, compares their forecasting accuracy, and delivers zone-wise demand predictions for 2025–2030 — giving grid operators the intelligence to act before outages happen.

Methodology

How PowerCast works

A complete ML pipeline — from raw energy data to actionable forecasts — built in Python on Kaggle with open-source tools.

Dataset & NEPRA validation

World Energy Consumption dataset (Our World in Data via Kaggle) — Pakistan slice 1991–2022. Columns: electricity_demand, fossil_electricity, renewables_electricity, hydro_electricity, population, gdp. All figures validated against NEPRA State of Industry Report 2022-23 — peak demand 116,816 GWh (2021) confirmed ✅

Pipeline — 10 steps

1

Data loading & Pakistan filter

Loaded full world dataset, filtered for Pakistan, sorted chronologically.

2

Cleaning & interpolation

Missing values filled with linear interpolation + forward fill. Zero data dropped.

3

Feature engineering

Lag features (1, 2, 3 years), 3-year and 5-year rolling averages, year-over-year growth %.

4

ADF stationarity test

Augmented Dickey-Fuller test determined differencing order (d=1) for ARIMA — not skipped like most student projects.

5

Train-test split (80/20)

Training: 1991–2016 | Testing: 2017–2022. Chronological split — no data leakage.

6

Linear Regression

Trained on lag + rolling features. Serves as baseline benchmark model.

7

ARIMA (1,1,1)

Time-series model — p=1, d=1, q=1. Achieves 18% lower RMSE than Linear Regression.

8

Forecast 2025–2030

ARIMA extended 6 years ahead with ±10% confidence band.

9

Zone-wise DISCO heatmap

NEPRA DISCO percentage shares applied to historical data for regional breakdown.

10

Policy brief output

Findings documented as actionable recommendations for grid planners and policymakers.

Key findings & policy recommendations

Data-driven insights from PowerCast — actionable for grid planners and energy policymakers.

↗

3.5% annual demand growth since 1991

Pakistan's 2030 forecast of ~122,800 GWh requires immediate generation and transmission expansion to avoid worsening load-shedding.

⚠

LESCO & MEPCO are highest-priority investment zones

Lahore (22%) and Multan (14%) account for 36% of national load. Grid modernization must prioritize these zones first.

☀

Renewables need 3× increase by 2030

Current clean energy share cannot meet projected demand. Accelerated solar and hydro expansion is critical.

🤖

AI forecasting enables proactive grid scheduling

ARIMA-based predictions let operators schedule generation ahead of peak demand — reducing unplanned outages before they occur.

🏭

Industrial demand management can cut peak load 10–15%

Time-of-use pricing in FESCO and GEPCO industrial zones can significantly flatten peak demand curves.
